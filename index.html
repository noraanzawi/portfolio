Nora Anzawi

Intro

I am a social entrepreneur focused on providing informed, comprehensive research analysis and data services to underserved communities. I help foundations, non-profits, and consultancies to access, curate, and analyze internal and secondary data to understand socioeconomic trends, support strategic goals, and increase stakeholder engagement.

My passion is to empower the public with sustainable processes, information, and tools they need to achieve their vision.

Services

Data Mining

Data exploration, application of quantitative and qualitative methods, reusable data analysis.

Geospatial Analysis

Harvest and analyze geographic data to uncover spatial patterns, trends, and relationships.

Data Visualization

Creation of static and interactive data visualizations for reports, dashboards, and presentations.

KC Connectivity Report

  • What is it: An in-depth analysis of Kansas City’s digital divide at the start of the 2020 pandemic, created with the City and school district and later templatized for customers.
  • How to view: The full interactive report is hosted on mySidewalk here.

AltCap Relief & Recovery Fund Report

  • What is it: An overview of the impact created by relief funding for small businesses in a simple, visually appealing format. Provided to funders, used for advocacy efforts, and presented to civic leaders engaged in AltCap's efforts as a CDFI.
  • How to view: The interactive report is available on the AltCap website here.

Greenville, SC Community Insights

  • What is it: Community insights dashboard customized and curated for the Greenville, SC Chamber of Commerce. The deliverable was shared with stakeholders as part of the Accelerate Greenville initiative to invest in data-oriented practices to address barriers to employment and talent retention.
  • How to view: A sample from the section Growing a Greater Greenville can be found in the Google Drive The full interactive dashboard can is available on the mySidewalk host site here.

KC Streetcar NorthRail Extension Maps

  • What is it: Visualization and funding scenario framework based on analysis of two potential transit corridors connecting downtown Kansas City with a neighboring jurisdiction. Includes alternatives analysis maps and funding assumptions based on public sources.
  • How to view: Maps available upon request here. Project inspired by work on a multidisciplinary team evaluating streetcar corridor extensions and funding strategies. All data presented here is anonymized or simulated; no proprietary information is included.

Growth, Equity & Individual Welfare — A CDFI Impact Framework

  • What is it: A research brief or one-pager provided to an array of stakeholders (general public, non-profits, think tanks, private partners) to prime the audience on the key themes included in the full publication.
  • How to view: The research brief is attached, containing a DOI link to the full publication on the Carolina Small Business Development Fund (CSBDF) website.
